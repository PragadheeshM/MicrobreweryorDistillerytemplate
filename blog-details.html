The Science Behind Hop Selection

Marcus Chen
· Apr 8, 2026 · 5 min read

Every great beer starts with a simple question: which hops? At Copper & Cask, hop selection is both an art and a science — a process that begins months before a single grain is mashed. In this post, I'll walk you through how we choose our hops and why it matters more than most people think.

Understanding Hop Terroir

Just like grapes in winemaking, hops are deeply affected by where they're grown. The soil composition, elevation, rainfall, and sunlight hours all contribute to the hop's alpha acid content, essential oil profile, and aromatic characteristics. A Cascade hop grown in Oregon's Willamette Valley will taste noticeably different from one grown in Washington's Yakima Valley — same variety, different personality.

"We don't just buy hops — we visit the farms, smell the bines, and talk to the growers. That relationship is what makes our beers unique."

Our Selection Process

Every year between August and September, I travel to hop farms across the Pacific Northwest for what the industry calls "hop selection." Here's our 4-step approach:

  1. 1
    Farm Visits — Walk the hop yards, inspect the bines for health and vigor.
  2. 2
    Rub & Sniff Tests — Crush hop cones to release oils and evaluate aroma intensity.
  3. 3
    Lab Analysis — Test alpha acids, beta acids, and essential oil content.
  4. 4
    Pilot Batches — Brew small test batches to understand real-world performance.

Why It Matters to You

The difference between a good beer and a great one often comes down to ingredient quality. By selecting hops at their peak freshness and from the best growing regions, we ensure that every Copper & Cask beer delivers the complex, layered flavors our patrons have come to expect. It's not just about bitterness — it's about balance, aroma, and that magical "one more sip" factor.

Next time you're at the taproom, ask your server about the hop profile of your beer. We're always happy to geek out about ingredients with fellow craft enthusiasts. And if you're really curious, join one of our brewery tours — I'll show you the hops up close and personal.

Cheers,
Marcus Chen, Head Brewer
